ðŸ›‘ Hate Speech Detection Project The Hate Speech Detection project involves building a machine learning model that identifies and classifies hateful, offensive, or abusive text, often on social media platforms.
Data Collection & Preprocessing
Datasets from platforms like Twitter, Reddit, or Facebook containing labeled text (e.g., hate speech, offensive, neutral).
Text Cleaning: Removing special characters, lowercasing, and handling stopwords.
Tokenization & Vectorization:
BERT Tokenizer for contextual embeddings.
CountVectorizer or TF-IDF for feature extraction (optional).
Model Building
Model: BERT, LSTM, or CNN.
Libraries: TensorFlow, Transformers, Scikit-Learn.
Training: Using pre-trained BERT with fine-tuning on labeled data.
Evaluation
Metrics: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score.
Confusion Matrix: To assess false positives and negatives.
Deployment
Backend: Django (for web integration).
Frontend: HTML/CSS/JS (for displaying results).
Model Prediction: Load the trained model (model.pkl) and tokenizer (tokenizer.pkl) for inference.

Hate speech refers to any form of communication (spoken, written, or visual) that demeans, threatens, or discriminates against individuals or groups based on attributes such as:

  • Race or ethnicity
  • Religion or belief system
  • Gender or sexual orientation
  • Disability or health condition
  • Political or social affiliation
